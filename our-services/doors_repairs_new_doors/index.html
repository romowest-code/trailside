    Interior Doors

    Interior doors are essential components of residential homes, serving both functional and aesthetic purposes.

    They provide privacy, control sound transmission between rooms, and contribute to the overall design aesthetic of a house. Here are some key points about residential interior doors:

    • Functionality: Interior doors separate living spaces, bedrooms, bathrooms, and other areas within a home, offering privacy and noise reduction.
    • Materials: Common materials include wood (solid or engineered), medium-density fiberboard (MDF), fiberglass, and metal. Each material offers different benefits in terms of durability, insulation, and style.
    • Styles: Interior doors come in various styles to match different home decors, from traditional panel doors to modern flush doors and everything in between.
    • Installation Types: They can be pre-hung (come with a frame) or slab (door only), and can be installed as swinging, sliding, bifold, or pocket doors depending on space requirements and preferences.
    • Customization: Many interior doors can be customized with different finishes, hardware, and glass inserts to complement the home’s interior design.

    Choosing the right interior doors can significantly impact the functionality, energy efficiency, and overall aesthetics of a residential space. Professional installation ensures proper fit and operation, enhancing the door’s performance and longevity.

    Storm Doors

    Storm doors are an essential addition to many homes, providing an extra layer of protection against the elements while also enhancing energy efficiency and curb appeal. Here are some common types of storm doors:

    • Full-View Storm Doors: These doors feature a large glass panel that extends the full length of the door, allowing maximum light and visibility. They often come with interchangeable glass and screen panels for seasonal adjustments.
    • Ventilating Storm Doors: These doors have retractable screens that can be rolled up or down, allowing for easy ventilation when desired. They’re great for letting in fresh air while keeping insects out.
    • Security Storm Doors: Built with durability in mind, these doors feature heavy-duty frames and multiple locking points. They often have metal grilles over the glass for added protection.
    • Retractable Screen Storm Doors: These innovative doors have a screen that can be completely hidden when not in use, providing an unobstructed view and seamless appearance.
    • Traditional Storm Doors: These classic doors typically have a partial glass panel on top and a solid panel on the bottom. They offer a good balance of light, ventilation, and privacy.

    Storm doors come in various materials including aluminum, vinyl, and wood, each offering different benefits in terms of durability, maintenance, and aesthetics. When choosing a storm door, consider factors such as climate, security needs, and your home’s architectural style to find the best fit.
